Three Prompts You Can Use Tomorrow

Prompt 1 — Challenge Weak Arguments
Use in ChatGPT or Gemini after generating an initial draft

"Your second argument is the weakest. What evidence would a skeptic demand? Strengthen it or replace it."

Prompt 2 — Steelman the Opposition
Forces students to sit with discomfort and engage opposing views

"Now give me the strongest possible counterargument to my thesis. Don't hold back."

Prompt 3 — Catch Hallucinations
The fact-checking moment — at least one source usually doesn't exist

"List every source you cited. For each one, give me the DOI or a direct URL so I can verify it."

"Using AI without documenting it is the new plagiarism."

Your Monday Morning Move

Step 1: Pick one assignment you give this semester.

Step 2: Paste its prompt into ChatGPT. If AI produces a passing submission in under 2 minutes, it's time to redesign.

Step 3: Add the requirement: "Submit your AI conversation link alongside your final work." That single change shifts the entire dynamic.
