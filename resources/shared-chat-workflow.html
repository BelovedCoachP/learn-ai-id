The "Shared Chat" Submission Workflow

A step-by-step guide for requiring and collecting AI conversation links as part of any LMS assignment.

Why require shared chat links? When students submit their AI conversation alongside their final work, AI becomes transparent and gradable. You can see their thinking process: how they prompted, what they questioned, where they revised. The conversation is the evidence of learning.

This guide covers the three tools featured in The Authenticity Lab and works with any LMS.

Part 1

How Students Share Their AI Conversations

Each tool handles sharing differently. Below are current steps for each platform, along with what students and faculty need to know.

ChatGPT — Shared Conversation Link

Free and Plus accounts • chat.openai.com
  1. Open the conversation the student wants to submit.
  2. Click the Share icon (arrow pointing up) in the top-right corner of the chat.
  3. In the modal, click "Copy link." The link creates a read-only snapshot of the conversation at the time of sharing.
  4. Paste the link into the LMS assignment submission (text entry field or URL submission).
https://chatgpt.com/share/abc123-example-link
/* Read-only snapshot — reviewer can see full conversation */
ChatGPT sharing details
Account required to view? No — anyone with the link can view the shared conversation without logging in.
Can the student edit after sharing? The shared link is a snapshot. If the student continues the conversation, the new messages will not appear in the shared link unless they re-share.
Free account limitations Sharing is available on free accounts. No Plus subscription required.
Faculty tip: Instruct students to share the link after they've finished their AI interaction. If they need to add more, they should generate a new share link and resubmit.

Gemini — Shared Conversation Link

Free and Advanced accounts • gemini.google.com
  1. Open the conversation in Gemini.
  2. Click the Share button (three dots menu or share icon at the top of the conversation).
  3. Select "Share" and then "Create public link."
  4. Copy the generated link and paste it into the LMS submission.
https://g.co/gemini/share/example-link-id
/* Public link — viewable by anyone */
Gemini sharing details
Account required to view? No — public links are viewable without a Google account.
Can the student edit after sharing? The shared link is a snapshot of the conversation at the time of sharing.
Workspace accounts Some institutional Google Workspace accounts may restrict sharing. Check with IT if students report issues.
Heads up: If your institution uses Google Workspace for Education, the admin may have disabled Gemini sharing. Test this before assigning. Have a backup plan: students can export the conversation as a PDF using the browser's Print → Save as PDF function.

NotebookLM — Shared Notebook Link

Free with Google account • notebooklm.google.com
  1. Open the notebook containing the student's sources and chat history.
  2. Click the Share button in the top-right corner of the notebook.
  3. Set sharing permissions to "Anyone with the link can view."
  4. Copy the link and paste it into the LMS submission.
https://notebooklm.google.com/notebook/example-id
/* Viewer can see uploaded sources + all chat interactions */
NotebookLM sharing details
Account required to view? Viewers need a Google account but do not need NotebookLM access to view a shared notebook.
What can the reviewer see? All uploaded sources (PDFs, docs, links) and the complete chat history with the AI about those sources.
Why this matters NotebookLM is "source-locked" — the AI can only reference materials the student uploaded. This lets you verify students engaged with your assigned readings.
Faculty tip: Require students to name their notebook clearly (e.g., "ENG 111 — Research Essay — [Student Name]") so you can quickly identify submissions when reviewing multiple links.
Part 2

Setting Up the Submission in Your LMS

These steps work across Canvas, Blackboard, Brightspace, Moodle, and other learning management systems. Adapt the specifics to your platform.

Option A: Text Entry Submission

Best for: assignments where students also write in the LMS
  1. Create your assignment and enable Text Entry as a submission type.
  2. In the assignment instructions, include a clearly labeled section asking students to paste their AI conversation link(s) at the top or bottom of their text entry.
  3. Provide a formatting template in the instructions (see the sample language below).

Option B: URL / Website URL Submission

Best for: collecting links as standalone deliverables
  1. Create your assignment and enable Website URL (or "URL submission") as a submission type.
  2. If your LMS allows multiple submission types on one assignment, enable both File Upload (for the final product) and URL Submission (for the chat link).
  3. If your LMS only allows one submission type, use Text Entry and have students paste links there, or create two linked assignments: one for the file, one for the link.

Option C: File Upload + Link in Document

Best for: traditional file upload workflows
  1. Keep your standard File Upload submission type.
  2. Require students to include their AI conversation link(s) as a clickable hyperlink on the first page or cover page of their submitted document.
  3. Add a "Process Documentation" section to your assignment template that students fill in before their main content.
Part 3

Copy-and-Paste Language for Your Assignments

Use or adapt these text blocks directly in your LMS assignment instructions and syllabus.

Assignment Instructions — AI Interaction Requirement

AI Documentation Required: This assignment requires you to use AI tools as part of your research and writing process. Along with your final submission, you must include shared conversation links from the AI tool(s) you used. These links allow me to see your full interaction with the AI — your prompts, the AI's responses, and how you refined your work.

What to submit:

1. Your final [essay / project / analysis]
2. A shared ChatGPT link and/or Gemini link showing your prompting and revision process
3. A shared NotebookLM notebook link showing your source interrogation (if applicable)
4. A brief reflection (150–300 words) on how you used AI and what you changed from the AI's suggestions

How to share your AI conversation: See the "Shared Chat Submission Guide" provided in our course materials for step-by-step instructions for each tool.

Syllabus Statement — AI Use Policy

In this course, artificial intelligence tools (including ChatGPT, Gemini, and NotebookLM) are permitted and, for designated assignments, required. AI is treated as a professional tool — similar to a calculator in a math course or a style guide in a writing course. You are expected to use AI transparently and critically.

For assignments that require AI documentation, you must submit shared conversation links alongside your final work. Your AI interaction will be graded as part of the assignment. Using AI without documenting it on a required-documentation assignment is an academic integrity violation, just as failing to cite a source would be.

You remain the author of your work. AI is a collaborator, not a ghostwriter. Your grade reflects your ability to prompt effectively, evaluate AI output critically, and synthesize a final product that reflects your own thinking.

Student FAQ — Quick Answers

Q: Do I need a paid account?
No. ChatGPT (free), Gemini (free), and NotebookLM (free with a Google account) all support conversation sharing.

Q: What if my institution blocks one of these tools?
Use whichever tools are available to you. If sharing links are blocked by your institution's settings, export your conversation as a PDF (Print → Save as PDF) and submit that instead.

Q: Can I use a different AI tool?
Check with your instructor. If the tool allows you to share or export a full conversation log, it may be acceptable. The key requirement is that your process must be visible.

Q: What if I didn't use AI for part of my work?
That's fine. Document what you did use AI for and note in your reflection which sections are entirely your own work.

Part 5

Troubleshooting & Common Issues

Student says "I can't find the share button"

Share interfaces update frequently. Direct students to search "[Tool Name] how to share conversation [current year]" for the latest instructions. As a universal backup, any conversation can be exported via the browser's Print → Save as PDF function.

Shared link doesn't work / shows "not found"

This usually means the student shared a link to their own chat view, not the public share link. Ask them to re-share using the dedicated Share button (not by copying the browser URL). The share link will have a distinct URL format that differs from the regular chat URL.

Institutional restrictions block sharing

If your college's Google Workspace or network policies block AI tool sharing features, work with IT to whitelist the relevant domains, or accept PDF exports of conversations as an alternative. Document this workaround in your assignment instructions.

Student submits a very short conversation

Set minimum expectations in your assignment: "Your conversation should include at least [X] rounds of prompting and revision." The Process-Oriented Rubric in the Assignment Redesign Worksheet grades conversation quality, which naturally incentivizes deeper engagement.
