Process-Oriented Rubric

Grade the learning journey, not just the final product. This rubric evaluates how students interact with AI as a transparent, documented part of the assignment.

Core principle: This rubric shifts assessment weight from the final deliverable to the documented process — how students prompted, questioned, fact-checked, and synthesized with AI tools. Adapt the weightings and descriptors to fit your discipline and assignment type.
Criterion Exemplary (A) Proficient (B) Developing (C) Insufficient (D/F)
Prompt Quality & Iteration 25% Conversation shows sophisticated, evolving prompts. Student challenges AI assumptions, narrows scope, and requests evidence. Clear progression from initial query to refined output. Multiple rounds of prompting with meaningful revisions. Student corrects at least one AI error or refines direction based on output quality. Some iteration visible, but prompts are mostly surface-level rewording. Limited evidence of critical engagement with AI responses. Single prompt with no revision. Conversation shows copy-paste behavior with no interrogation of AI outputs.
Source Interrogation 20% NotebookLM log shows deep, analytical questions about course materials. Student synthesizes across multiple sources and identifies contradictions or gaps in the literature. Meaningful questions asked of course sources. Student connects AI-generated insights back to specific assigned readings. Surface-level questions (e.g., "summarize this"). Minimal connection between AI output and course content. No evidence of engaging with course sources through AI tools, or required sources not uploaded to NotebookLM.
Fact-Checking & Hallucination Detection 15% Student identifies and documents specific AI errors or hallucinations. Provides corrections with cited evidence from course materials or credible external sources. Student notes at least one inaccuracy in AI output and provides a correction supported by a credible source. Student mentions AI might be wrong but provides no specific evidence or documented correction. No evidence of evaluating AI accuracy. AI output accepted at face value without question.
Human Synthesis & Original Thinking 25% Final product clearly extends beyond AI output. Student's own argument, analysis, or voice is dominant. AI served as a tool for exploration, not the author of the work. Final product shows meaningful human revision. Student's perspective is present alongside AI-assisted content. Final product is lightly edited AI output. Student voice is minimal and hard to distinguish from the AI's. Final product is indistinguishable from raw AI output. No evidence of human revision or original thought.
Reflection & Metacognition 15% Thoughtful reflection on how AI shaped the student's thinking. Articulates specific insights gained and what they would do differently in future AI-assisted work. Adequate reflection addressing how AI was used and at least one specific insight gained from the process. Generic reflection (e.g., "AI was helpful"). No specific insight about the learning process or personal growth. No reflection submitted, or reflection does not address the AI interaction or learning process.
Customization tips: Adjust the percentage weightings to reflect your priorities. For a research-heavy course, increase Source Interrogation. For a writing-intensive course, increase Human Synthesis. If you don't use NotebookLM, redistribute Source Interrogation weight across the other criteria. The total should always equal 100%.
